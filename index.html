We want to win.

AI at GSB exists to make sure Stanford MBAs get the best jobs, perform exceptionally in them, and lead organizations through what AI is changing about work. This memo lays out how we plan to do that over the next academic year.

01 · Our mission

What winning means.

We define success not by attendance or sentiment, but by what our classmates can do when they leave Stanford. Three outcomes anchor everything that follows.

i.

The best jobs.

GSB students place into the roles where AI capability is now a real criterion.

ii.

The best performers.

Once in role, our classmates outperform peers because they know how to apply these tools.

iii.

The best leaders.

They guide organizations through the opportunities and challenges of an AI-powered future.

02 · How we get there

Three pillars of AI readiness.

Mission translates into three concrete capabilities every member should leave with. Fluency is the language; agency is the build; perspectives is the thesis. Each pillar has its own definition, evaluation, and event types — every program we run ladders up to one of them.

Pillar 01

AI Fluency

Speak the language of AI.

Definition

Understanding the terminology and the basics of how AI works, including the technical evolution that got us here.

Components
  • Basics of AI and how an LLM works
  • The tech evolution that got us here
  • The AI value chain and production chain
  • Current buzzwords, used productively
How we evaluate
  • Members can hold a dinner-party conversation on AI
  • Members use current buzzwords in a productive way
Event types
Demos Foundational talks
Pillar 02

AI Agency

Build and apply AI to real problems.

Definition

An engineering mindset and the ability to apply AI to problems members see in their own lives and organizations. Curiosity, courage, competence.

Components
  • Identify a problem worth solving with tech
  • Navigate the latest tools to define a solution
  • Build, not just ideate
  • Troubleshoot using available resources
  • Measure and evaluate the result
How we evaluate
  • Members move from problem to solution to build
  • Members reflect on what worked and what did not
Event types
Workshops Hackathon Demos
Pillar 03

AI Perspectives

Develop a thesis on the future.

Definition

A macro view on how AI will reshape industries, roles, and society, and a developed point of view on what it means to lead in this era.

Components
  • What it means to lead in the age of AI
  • AI's impact on industry, roles, and society
  • How the world will shape AI's evolution
How we evaluate
  • Members have their own elevator response on AI in their industry
  • Members have enough perspective to ask the next questions
Event types
Panels and talks SGDs Show and tell Newsletter
03 · How we measure

Three signals across the year.

A baseline reading goes to administration after fall quarter, and a year-end report follows in spring. Surveys for sentiment, engagement for behavior, application for evidence.

Signal i.

Student surveys

Recurring read on how prepared members feel to lead in the AI era.

  • TALK-style Google survey in-room at events
  • Post-graduation survey of the '26 class once they have started jobs
  • Can members articulate what AI at GSB is?
Signal ii.

Engagement

Attendance and continuity across the year, not just one-time turnout.

  • Signups versus attendance, with room-count estimates
  • Builder series continuity, via TALK-style punchcard or certificate
  • Series-over-series retention for perspectives and SGDs
Signal iii.

Skill application

Evidence that learning translates into coursework, recruiting, and internships.

  • Resource hub clicks and downloads
  • Member-built artifacts and LinkedIn posts
  • Self-reported use in classes, recruiting, and internships

07 · What we want to discuss

Open questions for administration.

We are surfacing these deliberately. They are areas where we want to test ideas with you before we lock them in.

i.

Engagement incentives.

How do we incentivize survey participation and sustained attendance? Options on the table include swag, a TALK-style punchcard, certificates, or tokens. We would like to align with administration on what feels appropriate.

ii.

Reach beyond our cohort.

How do we build community outside our immediate scholar group? We are exploring liaisons with the industry clubs as one mechanism, and would value administration's perspective on which clubs to prioritize.

iii.

Faculty and alumni network.

We want to build a roster organized by topic so we can tap experts quickly when current events break. We would value the administration's help making introductions and confirming who is in scope.

iv.

Alumni Day & CMC partnership.

Should AI at GSB run workshops at Alumni Day? How should we partner with the CMC on industry-specific AI prep for interns and post-grads — and on what timeline?
